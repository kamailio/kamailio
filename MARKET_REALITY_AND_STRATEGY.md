## Why Big Companies CAN'T Serve Your Market

### 1. Their Cost Structure Makes Small Deals Unprofitable

**Ribbon/Ericsson/Cisco Sales Process:**
```
Step 1: Initial meeting (2 sales engineers × 4 hours)
Step 2: Requirements gathering (1 week, 3 people)
Step 3: Custom proposal (solution architect × 3 days)
Step 4: Proof of concept (2 engineers × 2 weeks)
Step 5: Legal review (1 week)
Step 6: C-level approval (2 weeks)
Step 7: Implementation (team of 5 × 1 month)
Step 8: Training (1 week on-site)

Internal cost: $150,000-300,000
Minimum deal size to be profitable: $500,000+

For a $5,000 deal? Impossible.
For a $50,000 deal? Still loses money.
```

**Your Process:**
```
Step 1: Email conversation (1 hour)
Step 2: Demo call (30 minutes)
Step 3: Price quote (15 minutes)
Step 4: Setup (2-4 hours)
Step 5: Customer live

Your cost: 4-6 hours of your time
Profitable at: $2,000+ (and up)

You can profitably serve customers they ignore.
```

### 2. They Can't Customize (You Can)

**Enterprise Vendor:**
- Standard product only
- Customization requires: Change request → Product management review → Engineering backlog → 6-12 month wait
- Minimum custom development: $100,000
- Customer gets generic solution that's "close enough"

**You:**
- Customer needs custom IVR for their specific workflow?
  - You build it in 2 days
  - Charge $3,000
  - Customer gets exactly what they need

**Real Example:**
```
Customer: "We need our PBX to integrate with our proprietary CRM system."

Big Vendor: "That's not a standard feature. We can add it to the roadmap.
            Custom integration would be $150,000 and take 6 months."

You: "I can have that working in a week for $5,000."

Guess who gets the customer?
```

### 3. They're Too Slow (You're Fast)

**Big company deployment timeline:**
- Sales cycle: 3-6 months
- Procurement: 1-2 months
- Implementation: 2-6 months
- Total: 6-14 months

**You:**
- Sales: 1-2 calls
- Setup: Same week
- Live: Days, not months

**Customer perspective:**
```
Scenario: Business needs phone system NOW

Big Vendor: "We can have you live in Q3 2026"
You: "You'll be live by Friday"

Speed is a competitive advantage.
```

### 4. They Don't Care About Small Customers

**What happens when a 50-person company has an issue:**

**With Ribbon/Cisco:**
- Opens ticket
- Gets level 1 support (reading scripts)
- Takes 2-3 days to escalate
- "Working as designed" or "Will be fixed in next major release"
- Customer feels ignored (because they are)

**With you:**
- Emails you directly
- You respond in hours
- You actually fix it
- You might even add features they need
- Customer feels valued (because they are)

**This matters more than you think.**

### 5. They Sell Products (You Solve Problems)

**Big company approach:**
```
"Here's our product. It has 10,000 features.
 Here's a 500-page manual. Good luck."
```

**Your approach:**
```
"What problem are you trying to solve?
 Let me build exactly what you need."
```

**Customers don't want products. They want solutions to their specific problems.**

You can provide that. They can't (profitably).
